# Book: Monolith to Microservices (O'Reilly)

## Chapter 1: Just Enough Microservices

### Microservice Definition

Microservices are **independently deployable services** modeled around
a business domain. They communicate with each other via networks.
It follows that a microservice architecture is based on multiple collaborating
microservices.

Microservices are a type of **Service-Oriented Architecture (SOA)**, albeit one that
is "opinionated" about how service boundaries should be drawn, and that
**independent deployability is key**.

Microservices expose the business capabilities that they encapsulate via one
ore more **network endpoints**. Microservices communicate with each other via
these networks, making them a form of **distributed system**.
